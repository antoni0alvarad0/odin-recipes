Pikliz Recipe

This bright and fiery Haitian condiment (pronounced "pick-lees") is traditionally served with meats and fried food to balance rich flavors.

Pikliz (it’s pronounced PEEkleez) is Haiti’s all-purpose condiment — it’s hot sauce, but also its coleslaw and kraut. You use its crunch and tang to tame fritay, which can be griot (pork fried in its own fat), tassot (fried beef) or even banan peze (pressed and fried sweet plantains). Or if your're eating a bowl of soup or rice and beans you can simply drizzle the liquid from the pikliz jar.

Ingredients

  • 3 Scotch Bonnet Peppers
  • 3 cup of cabbage grated
  • 1 cup of carrot grated
  • 3 cups of garlic crushed
  • 4 whole cloves
  • 2 Tbsp white sugar
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 2 cup of whit vinegar

Instructions

  1. Thinly slice all the ingredients.
  2. Place all the ingredients in an airtight jar, and fill with the white vinegar.
  3. Let refrigerate for at least 24 hours before using.
  4. Enjoy along with any type of protien, I recommend a some really nice sea food.
